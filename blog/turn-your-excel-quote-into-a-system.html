Turn Your Excel Quote Into a Real Quoting System

A lot of contractors have a good spreadsheet. That is not the problem. The problem is that the spreadsheet still makes you do too much work every time a new lead turns into a quote.

Your spreadsheet has business knowledge

It probably contains your standard services, rough production rates, material assumptions, notes, and the line items you trust. Do not throw that away. Use it as the base for a cleaner system.

Separate pricing from formatting

In Excel, pricing and presentation often live in the same place. That is why changing one thing can break the layout. A real quoting system keeps your saved items separate from the client-facing quote.

Save the things you repeat

Start with your top 25 line items. Add the rooms you quote most often. Add standard terms, deposit notes, warranty language, and upgrade options. Once those pieces are saved, each quote becomes assembly work instead of rebuilding.

Keep the contractor judgment

The goal is not to make quoting automatic. The goal is to make quoting faster while keeping your experience in the driver's seat.
