Do You Know What Your Last Job Actually Cost?

Most contractors know the quote total. Fewer know the real final cost. Materials get picked up in multiple runs, small receipts disappear, refunds get forgotten, and the job that felt profitable might have been thinner than it looked.

The problem is not just spending

The problem is matching spending to the right job. A contractor might buy lumber for one project, tile for another, and a tool for the shop in the same week. If those purchases are not tagged clearly, job costing turns into guesswork.

Start with a simple review

At minimum, compare the quote against material purchases after the job wraps. Group Home Depot Pro purchases, supplier invoices, subcontractor costs, and refunds. Even a rough review will show where your pricing needs tightening.

The future is less receipt chasing

The long-term goal is simple: when you make a purchase, QuoteDr should help you assign it to the right active job. That way you can see job cost while the work is happening, not a month later when the lesson is already expensive.
